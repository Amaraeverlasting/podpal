FAQ

Common questions.

Does PodPal hear my guest, or just me? +

Right now PodPal uses your microphone directly. For remote recordings (Zoom, Riverside, Squadcast), it hears your voice only. Dual-source capture - so PodPal hears both sides of the conversation - is our #1 feature in development.

Does it work with Zoom, Riverside, Squadcast? +

Yes. PodPal runs in a browser tab alongside your recording software. Open it on a second monitor, start listening, then record as normal in Zoom or Riverside. They don't interfere with each other.

Is my audio uploaded or stored anywhere? +

No audio is uploaded or stored. Transcription happens locally in your browser using the Web Speech API. Only the text is sent to our AI research layer - never the raw audio. Your episodes stay yours.

What browser does it work on? +

Chrome and Edge work best. Safari has partial support. Firefox is not supported yet. PodPal works on Mac, Windows, and Linux. Mobile support is in progress.

How is this different from just using ChatGPT before my show? +

ChatGPT is preparation. PodPal is presence. No amount of pre-show research helps when your guest takes the conversation somewhere unexpected - which they always do. PodPal responds to what's actually being said, right now. It's the difference between a briefing document and a researcher sitting beside you during the interview.

What if my internet drops mid-episode? +

Transcription keeps running locally. Research cards pause until connection restores, then catch up automatically. Your recording in Riverside or Zoom is completely unaffected.

Can I cancel anytime? +

Yes. No contracts, no lock-in. Cancel from your account settings anytime and you won't be billed again. Beta subscribers keep their $19/month price for as long as they stay subscribed - but there's no penalty for leaving.
