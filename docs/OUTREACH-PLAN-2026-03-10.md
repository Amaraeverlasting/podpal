### CATHY HACKL REFERRAL ENGINE

This is the highest-leverage move after Wednesday. One well-positioned ask to Cathy can put PodPal in front of her entire audience.

**What to ask Cathy (during or right after the session):**

1. **Share one thing from the session** - a screenshot of PodPal running, or one observation about what it surfaced. Keep it low-effort for her. Have the screenshot ready and offer it to her directly.

2. **Draft a post for her** - give her something she can literally copy-paste or lightly edit. Make it easy. Something like:
   ```
   Just had a conversation with Mic Mann (@micmannsa) on Exponential Africa Podcast. He was running a tool called PodPal in real time during the interview - live research on screen while we talked. First time I've experienced that as a guest. Interesting.
   podpal.show
   ```
   Keep it neutral and honest. Don't make it sound like an ad.

3. **Ask if she'd mention it on her next appearance** - not a formal request, just a casual "if it comes up and feels natural." One mention from Cathy on a podcast reaches a different audience entirely.

4. **Give her a referral link or code** - CATHY2026 is already set up. If she wants to share it with her network, she has something concrete to offer.

**What not to do:**
- Don't ask Cathy to endorse a product she's used once
- Don't write a fake quote and ask her to post it
- Don't ask for anything the day of the session - let the session breathe

**Timeline:**
- Wednesday session: focus on the interview
- Wednesday night or Thursday: thank her, share the show notes PodPal generated, and then one ask
- Keep the ask light. The relationship matters more than one post.
